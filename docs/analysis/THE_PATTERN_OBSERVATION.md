# The Pattern: Observations on Threat Intelligence Economics

**Document Date:** April 21, 2026
**Author:** WinterGate Intelligence Collective
**Classification:** Public Market Analysis

---

## PREAMBLE

This document presents observed patterns in the commercial threat intelligence industry. It does not allege intent, conspiracy, or coordination. It simply notes what any independent observer can see: a series of market behaviors that, taken together, form a recognizable pattern.

Readers are encouraged to draw their own conclusions.

---

## SECTION 1: THE OBSERVATION

### 1.1 What Anyone Can See

Over the past decade, the commercial threat intelligence market has exhibited certain consistent characteristics:

| Characteristic | Observation |
|----------------|-------------|
| Pricing | Remains uniformly high across vendors ($30k-150k/year) |
| Data Access | Restricted behind paywalls despite being derived from public sources |
| Update Frequency | Delayed 24-72 hours, even for automated data |
| Format | Proprietary, making switching costly |
| Contracts | Multi-year commitments standard |
| Transparency | Algorithms and methodologies are trade secrets |

### 1.2 What Makes It Notable

These characteristics are not unique to threat intelligence. They appear in other markets with certain structural features. What makes this market worth observing is the combination:

- The raw data (IP addresses, attack patterns) is often public
- The collection methods are not proprietary (anyone can run a honeypot)
- The marginal cost of distribution approaches zero
- Yet prices remain high and access remains restricted

## SECTION 2: THE NUMBERS

### 2.1 Cost to Produce vs. Price to Purchase

| Component | Estimated Production Cost | Typical Market Price |
|-----------|-------------------------|---------------------|
| IP Blacklist (5,000 IPs) | Negligible (automated) | $10k-30k/year |
| MITRE ATT&CK Mapping | Low (public framework) | Often extra fee |
| ASN/ISP Mapping | Low (public databases) | Often extra fee |
| Attack Telemetry | Low (honeypot network) | $50k-100k/year |

### 2.2 The Gap

Between production cost and market price exists a gap. This gap is not unique to this industry. It exists in many industries with certain characteristics: high switching costs, limited transparency, and customer bases that assume complexity justifies price.

## SECTION 5: POSSIBLE EXPLANATIONS

### 5.1 Legitimate Explanations

There are many legitimate reasons for commercial pricing:

| Explanation | Validity |
|-------------|----------|
| Enterprise support and SLAs | Legitimate value-add |
| Compliance and certification | Legitimate requirement |
| Integration with existing tools | Legitimate need |
| Vendor liability and insurance | Legitimate cost |
| Research and development | Legitimate investment |

### 5.2 Other Possible Explanations

There may be other factors. This document does not assert them. The reader is invited to consider:

- Network effects of proprietary formats
- Switching costs embedded in contracts
- Information asymmetry between vendor and customer
- Marketing expenditure as a barrier to entry

These are economic observations. Not allegations.
